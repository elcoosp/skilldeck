When you use a cloud‑based AI coding assistant, your code leaves your machine. Every prompt, every file you reference, every fix you request is sent to a third‑party server. For many developers and organizations, that’s a non‑starter.

## The Privacy Problem with Cloud AI

### Your Code Becomes Training Data
Most cloud AI providers reserve the right to use your prompts and code to train future models. Your proprietary algorithms, internal APIs, and business logic could inadvertently become part of a public model. Once that happens, you can’t take it back.

### Compliance and Legal Risks
If you work in finance, healthcare, or any regulated industry, sending code to the cloud may violate compliance requirements (GDPR, HIPAA, SOC2, etc.). Even if you’re a solo developer, you may have clients who expect their data not to be shared with third parties.

### Lack of Control
You don’t know where your data is stored, who has access, or how long it’s retained. Cloud providers change their terms, deprecate features, and adjust pricing – all outside your control.

## How Local AI Solves This

SkillDeck takes a fundamentally different approach: **everything runs on your machine**. Your code never leaves your computer unless you explicitly choose to share it.

- **No data exfiltration** – All processing happens locally. No network calls to external AI services (unless you configure an API‑based provider, which you control).
- **You own the keys** – API keys for optional cloud providers are stored in your OS keychain, never in SkillDeck’s database.
- **Transparent by design** – You can inspect exactly what data is being sent and where. There are no hidden telemetry or analytics.
